Screener Documentation

This page explains the core concepts used in the Intelligent Stock Screener Dashboard. It is for educational purposes.

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio

The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio compares a company’s share price to its earnings per share (EPS). It is often called a “multiple” because it shows how many dollars investors are willing to pay for one dollar of current earnings. In formula form, P/E = current share price ÷ earnings per share.

In the screener, a lower P/E can be interpreted as relatively “cheap” compared with peers or the market, while a higher P/E can signal that the stock is priced for stronger growth or potentially overvalued. The dashboard highlights conditions where the P/E appears cheap or expensive relative to a threshold you define, so you can quickly see valuation at a glance.

Support and Resistance

Support and resistance are price levels where the market has historically paused or reversed. Support is an area where a downtrend tends to slow or bounce because demand increases. Resistance is an area where an uptrend tends to stall because supply increases.

The screener uses recent price history and moving averages to approximate key zones where price has reacted. These areas help you understand whether the current price is pressing into resistance, bouncing from support, or trading in the middle of a range, which ties directly into your risk and reward planning.

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes to identify overbought or oversold conditions. RSI is plotted on a scale from 0 to 100. Readings above 70 are often interpreted as overbought, while readings below 30 are often seen as oversold.

In this screener, RSI helps you quickly see whether a stock has recently experienced strong buying pressure or heavy selling pressure. Oversold conditions can align with potential support zones, while overbought conditions can cluster near resistance. Combining RSI with other indicators like moving averages can improve the quality of trade setups.

EMA and SMA (Moving Averages)

Moving averages smooth price data to reveal the underlying trend. A Simple Moving Average (SMA) gives equal weight to all observations in the lookback window. An Exponential Moving Average (EMA) gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new information.

The screener computes moving averages such as SMA and EMA over configurable windows to determine whether price is trending above or below these reference levels. For example, being above a medium term SMA can signal bullish momentum, while trading below it can indicate weakness. These signals are combined with RSI and P/E to build a more complete technical and valuation picture.

Price Action

Price action is simply the movement of a security’s price over time as plotted on a chart. It is the raw data from which most technical indicators are derived. Many short term traders focus primarily on price action patterns such as breakouts, trendlines, and candlestick formations to make trading decisions.

In the screener, price action shows up through recent highs and lows, trend direction, and how price behaves around support, resistance, and moving averages. Rather than replacing your judgment, the dashboard is designed to summarize these behaviours so you can more easily decide whether a setup fits your trading plan.
